Understanding Cyber Threats

Cyber threats are evolving every day. Here are the most common ones you should know about:

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages designed to steal personal information. Avoid clicking on suspicious links or providing sensitive information to unknown sources.
  • Ransomware: Malicious software that locks your data until a ransom is paid. Always back up your data and avoid downloading files from unverified sources.
  • Social Engineering: Tactics used to manipulate individuals into revealing sensitive information. Be cautious when sharing personal details and verify the identity of individuals requesting sensitive data.
  • Malware: Harmful software that compromises devices and networks. Install reliable antivirus software and keep your system updated to defend against malware.
  • Zero-Day Exploits: Vulnerabilities in software that hackers exploit before developers can fix them. Keep your software and systems up to date to reduce risk.

Protecting Yourself Online

Follow these best practices to stay secure:

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all your accounts and store them securely in a password manager.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) for an extra layer of security.
  • Be cautious of links, email attachments, and messages from unknown sources.
  • Keep your devices and software updated to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Back up important data regularly to an external drive or cloud storage.
  • Secure your home Wi-Fi network with a strong password and encryption settings.
  • Educate yourself and others about common cyber threats and scams.
